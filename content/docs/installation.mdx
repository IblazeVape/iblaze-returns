title: Installation

---

## 1. Install the app

Install Reflow from the Shopify App Store like any other app. You'll be
asked to approve the standard permissions Reflow needs to read orders and
customer accounts — Reflow never writes to your product catalogue or
theme.

## 2. Connect your storefront

Once installed, Reflow automatically syncs with your Shopify orders and
customer accounts. There's no theme code to paste in and nothing to touch
in your Shopify admin beyond the initial app install.

## 3. Point customers at your portal

Your portal is available at a Reflow-hosted URL by default. On paid plans
you can point a custom subdomain (for example `returns.yourstore.com`) at
it instead — see [Customization](/docs/customization#custom-domain) for
how to set that up.

Add a link to your portal from your storefront's account page, order
confirmation emails, or footer — wherever your customers currently look
for order help.

## 4. Set your return window

Before you go live, set your return window in the admin (see
[Customization](/docs/customization#return-window)). The default is 30
days from delivery, matching Shopify's own default, but you can set
anywhere from 1 to 90 days.

That's it — no code, no theme changes, no separate hosting to manage.
